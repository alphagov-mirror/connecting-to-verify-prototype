Design and test your service using GOV.UK Verify

Find out how to design your service to work with GOV.UK Verify.

Where GOV.UK Verify fits in your service’s user journey

  • You should send the user to GOV.UK Verify at the point when you need to check who they are.
  • GOV.UK Verify will ask the user to choose a certified company. The certified company will verify the user’s identity and send them back to your service.
  • The user can then continue to use your service. You’ll have access to the user’s name, address and date of birth.

You can see how GOV.UK Verify works with a LOA 1 service and LOA 2 service.

If a user can’t be verified

You need to give the GOV.UK Verify team information about any other ways users can complete the service, for example by post or by phone. GOV.UK Verify will show this information to a user if their identity can’t be checked by a certified company.

Customise the content on GOV.UK Verify

You will be able to add specific content that will be displayed during the user’s journey on GOV.UK Verify.

You’ll need to be able to say:

  • why your service needs to verify users’ identities
  • what other ways users can complete the transaction

Test with your users

If a user can’t successfully verify they’ll need to be able to send you additional identification.

Create a reusable link to integrate your service prototype with GOV.UK Verify and test with users.

Assessing your service

If your service isn’t being assessed by GDS, you’ll still need to share how GOV.UK Verify fits in your service. This is to check there aren’t any security issues with the way you’ve built your service.
