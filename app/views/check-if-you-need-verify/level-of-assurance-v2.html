Choose the right level of assurance

You can choose how thorough you want your service's identity checks to be by picking a 'level of assurance'.

Which one you pick depends on:

  • how confident your service needs to be that a user is who they say they are
  • how much money your organisation could lose or how much damage could be done to its reputation if your service is threatened or attacked

To help make your choice, you should also think about:

  • how many users can use your service and how well-known it is - popular services are more likely to be threatened or attacked
  • if your service has been targeted by fraud or cyber crime in the past
  • what harm a user could do if they impersonated someone else to use your service

Level 1

Level 1 is suitable for most services that give users access to personal information.

Example The Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) use level 1 in their view driving licence information service. This is because users can get information from this service, but not use it to apply for anything more valuable or change their details.

If a user impersonated someone else to use this service, they wouldn't be able to do anything to harm that person.

Level 2

Level 2 will let you carry out more thorough identity checks. You should use level 2 if:

  • your service gives users benefits or money
  • your service lets users update information about themselves
  • you need to be able to take users to court if they fraudulently use your service

Example The Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) use level 2 in their claim for redundancy and monies owed service. This is because users can get money from this service.

If a user impersonated someone else to use this service, they could get money they're not entitled to. This could also stop the real user from being paid money they're owed.
