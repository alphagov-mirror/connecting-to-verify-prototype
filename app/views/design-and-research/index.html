Design your service using GOV.UK Verify

You service should send users to GOV.UK Verify at the point you need to check their identity. For most services, this will be near the start of the user journey.

Sign up journey

The user will choose a certified company to verify their identity. Once they’ve been verified, they’ll be sent back to your service. You’ll be able to see their name, address and date of birth.

Sign in journey

It will take less than a minute to sign in to your service.

How to send users to GOV.UK Verify from your service

You should let your users know they’re leaving your service before you send them to GOV.UK Verify. GDS has created different design patterns to help you do this.

If users can sign in to your service in more than one way

Use the multiple sign in page pattern if there’s more than one way for users to sign in to your service. For example, users can sign in to some HM Revenue and Customs services with either GOV.UK Verify or Government Gateway.

You can also use the create an account pattern to show new users the different ways to use your service.

If users can only sign in to your service with GOV.UK Verify

Use the single sign on pattern if there’s only one way to use your service.

If a user can’t be verified

GOV.UK Verify will show any users who can’t be verified the other ways they can use your service, for example by phone or by post.

See an example of what GOV.UK Verify shows a user who can’t be verified.
