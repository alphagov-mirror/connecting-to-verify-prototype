Choose the right level of assurance

GOV.UK Verify offers 2 different levels of assurance (LOA). Which one you pick depends on how sure you need to be your users are who they say they are.

LOA 1

You can use LOA 1 if there aren’t any serious consequences to a user impersonating someone else when using your service.

Example The Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) use LOA1 in their view driving licence information service. This is because users can only get information from this service. They can’t use it to apply for anything more valuable or change their details.

LOA 2

If there are serious consequences to a user impersonating someone else when using your service, you’ll need LOA 2.

You should also use LOA 2 if you need to able to take users to court when they give fraudulent information in your service.

Example HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) use LOA 2 in their file Self Assessment tax return service. This is because a user could be fined if someone else impersonates them and uses this service to give incorrect information to HMRC.

If you need a higher level of assurance

You’ll need a higher level of assurance if you need to be sure ‘beyond reasonable doubt’ that the user is who they say they are, for example if they need to show they’re physically identical to the person they claim to be.

Contact the GOV.UK Verify team if you think you might need a higher level of assurance for your service.
