You need to decide how your service will match identities with records, as well as what counts as a successful match.

You also need to decide what happens when:

  • an identity is successfully matched to a record in your database
  • an identity is matched to more than one record in your database
  • no match is found

This is known as deciding a matching strategy.

It's possible an identity that's been verified by a certified company will be slightly different to a record in the database. You should also look for records that are a close match and decide what you'll do with them.

Example Jonathan McDonald is successfully verified by a certified company. The service he wants to use tries to match this verified identity against a record in their database.

The only record they find is for a Johnathan MacDonald. This is the same user, but the identity and record don't match because their name is spelt differently.

There are often spelling mistakes in this particular database because the data is collected over the phone. The service applies 'fuzzy matching' rules to decide Jonathan McDonald is the same person as the Johnathan MacDonald in the database.

This match is successful and Jonathan McDonald can continue to use the service.
